# ABOUT ME — How to Use the About Me Database

## Overview
MJ's personal context now lives in the **About Me database** on the Dashboard page in Notion (ID: 36520c51-aebe-80ac-8ce6-cd30b4e6aa9c). Claude reads this at the start of every session. This file explains how to use that data.

## How to Read the About Me Database
Each row is a Q&A entry. Properties: Quiz (the question/topic), Answer (MJ's response), Type (category).

Categories:
- Who I Am 
personality, hobbies, social life, physical habits, decompress style
- How I Actually Work 
Work style, struggles, productivity patterns, momentum killers
- Life Priorities
What matters most to MJ, ranked or described
- Schedule Personality
packed vs breathing room, rest blocks, work/personal ratio preferences

## How Claude Should Use This Data
- Work tasks must never dominate the schedule by default. About Me data is equally as important as Projects data.
- Use Life Priorities to decide what earns a place on a heavy day — physical health, family time, and creative work are explicitly stated priorities.
- Use How I Actually Work to understand MJ's rhythms — he works in intense bursts then needs recovery. Don't overschedule after a heavy period.
- Use Schedule Personality to calibrate the day's intensity and balance.
- Use Who I Am to generate relevant personal task suggestions (gym, reading, music, runs) that match MJ's actual life — not generic wellness tasks.
- If MJ seems to be heading toward something that conflicts with his own stated priorities, flag it.
