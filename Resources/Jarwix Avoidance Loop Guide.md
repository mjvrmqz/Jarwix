# Jarwix Guide — Avoidance Loop

## Overview

This guide explains what happens when you avoid difficult work and how to reliably break the avoidance loop.

It applies to any task:

- Editing  
- Coding  
- Planning  
- Admin work  
- Creative work  
- Business tasks  

The goal is not motivation.  
The goal is **restoring execution when the brain enters avoidance mode**.

---

## 1. What’s actually happening (the avoidance loop)

When you face a difficult task, your brain runs a fast automatic process:

### Step A — Prediction
The task is interpreted as:
- difficult  
- unclear  
- long  
- risky (may fail or be done incorrectly)  

---

### Step B — Cost spike
Emotional + cognitive load feels too high, even if objectively it isn’t.

---

### Step C — Uncertainty spike
The brain generates:
- “I don’t know what to do next”
- “Where do I even start?”

---

### Step D — Escape option appears
The brain offers high-reward alternatives:
- scrolling  
- social media  
- sexual stimulation  
- anything low effort / high dopamine  

---

### Step E — State switch
Instead of consciously deciding, behavior shifts automatically into avoidance.

---

### Key insight
> Avoidance is not a decision. It is a default system under perceived stress.

---

## 2. The goal of this system

Not to eliminate discomfort.

The goal is:

> **Prevent avoidance from becoming the default response to discomfort.**

We do this by interrupting the loop early.

---

## 3. The 3-layer interception system

You do NOT try to “force discipline.”

You intervene at three specific points.

---

## STEP 1 — Identify the avoidance type (fast classification)

When stuck, label the state immediately:

### Types of avoidance

**Uncertainty avoidance**  
→ “I don’t know what to do next”

**Activation avoidance**  
→ “I know what to do, I just don’t want to start”

**Evaluation avoidance**  
→ “I’m afraid I’ll do it wrong / mess it up”

This step matters because each type requires a different correction.

---

## STEP 2 — Remove ambiguity before action

Avoidance thrives in unclear structure.

Before doing anything, you convert the situation into clarity.

You define:

- What does “done” look like?
- What is the next physical action (not the full plan)?
- What would 20% progress look like in visible form?

If you cannot answer this, your brain will default to escape.

---

### Key rule

> If the next step is not obvious, do not attempt “work.”  
> First create clarity.

---

## STEP 3 — Execute a “no-meaning entry action”

You do NOT try to “be productive.”

You only perform:

> A 2–5 minute action that creates contact with the task

### Properties of this action:

- Inside the real workflow  
- Too small to feel overwhelming  
- Too real to feel pointless  
- Requires zero commitment beyond starting  

---

### Examples

- Open the project and locate last state  
- Skim current progress and highlight issues  
- Write a “NEXT STEPS” section  
- List unknowns / blockers  
- Load tools/files required for work  
- Watch last 30–60 seconds of prior work  

---

### Purpose

Not progress.

Just:

> Break psychological avoidance by making contact with the task real.

---

## STEP 4 — Continue only after contact

After Step 3:

You are now allowed to choose:
- Continue working  
- Stop  

But only after entry has occurred.

---

### Rule

> No decision-making before contact with the task.

This prevents the brain from escaping before engagement begins.

---

## 4. Core principle of the system

This system is built on one idea:

> Avoidance is not caused by lack of discipline, but by ambiguity + high perceived activation cost.

So we do not fight motivation.

We:

- Remove ambiguity  
- Lower entry cost  
- Force early contact  
- Prevent escape before engagement begins  

---

## 5. What this system applies to

This works for any situation where you experience:

- Procrastination  
- Freezing  
- Avoidance  
- Task anxiety  
- “I don’t know where to start”  

It is universal because it targets **state mechanics, not specific tasks**.
