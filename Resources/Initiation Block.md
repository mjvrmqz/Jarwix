# Jarwix Guide — Approach Inhibition Loop

## Overview

This guide explains what happens when the correct next action is known, but cannot be initiated due to emotional resistance.

It applies to tasks involving:
- outreach
- calls
- messaging clients
- rejection risk
- social evaluation
- high-stakes communication

The goal is not to reduce fear.

The goal is to **decouple execution from emotional forecasting**.

---

## 1. What’s actually happening

This is not uncertainty-based avoidance.

This is **approach inhibition under known action**.

---

### Step A — Correct action is identified
The brain already knows:
- what must be done
- what step comes next

---

### Step B — Outcome simulation triggers
The brain simulates:
- rejection
- awkwardness
- failure
- negative evaluation

---

### Step C — Threat response activation
Even though no physical danger exists, the system treats it as social threat.

---

### Step D — Execution block
Initiation is suppressed despite clarity.

---

## Key insight

> The problem is not knowing what to do. The problem is the brain treating initiation as exposure to risk.

---

## 2. The goal of this system

Not to remove fear.

Not to increase confidence.

The goal is:

> **Separate execution from emotional prediction.**

---

## 3. The 3-layer intervention system

---

## STEP 1 — Label the inhibition state

When stuck, identify:

- “I know the action but cannot start”

This is approach inhibition, not uncertainty.

---

## STEP 2 — Collapse outcome simulation

Before acting, explicitly stop evaluating outcomes.

Replace thinking with:
- “only execution step matters”
- “no prediction allowed before action”

---

## STEP 3 — Execute micro-contact entry

Perform a 2–5 minute initiation action that does NOT include outcome exposure.

Examples:
- open outreach list only
- write draft message without sending
- open call script / template
- prepare first sentence only
- load CRM / contact list

---

## STEP 4 — Separate execution from exposure

Execution is allowed without commitment to completion.

Sending / calling is a second-step action, not part of initiation.

---

## 4. Core principle

Approach inhibition is not solved by motivation.

It is solved by:

> **splitting initiation from exposure to risk.**

---

## 5. Applies to

- outreach
- client communication
- sales calls
- messaging
- any socially evaluative task
